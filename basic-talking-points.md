# Security Awareness Primer (1 Hour)


# Session Objectives (5 minutes)

By the end of this session, employees should:

- Understand how social engineering targets people
- Recognize emotional manipulation (RICE)
- Identify high-risk scenarios in our environment
- Know what to do when something feels suspicious
- Understand our safe harbor obligations regarding client DMS data

Reinforce:
Security awareness protects:
- Our clients
- Their customers
- Our contracts
- Our reputation
- Each other

---

# 1. Our Risk Landscape (10 minutes)

## Who We Are

We are a second-party partner with access to:
- Client DMS data
- Consumer lead data
- Campaign analytics
- Platform credentials
- Vendor billing and ad spend systems

## Why That Matters

Client DMS data may include:
- Vehicle ownership information
- Service records
- Customer contact information
- Transaction histories

Under safe harbor expectations:
- We are trusted custodians
- Improper exposure impacts our clients, not just us
- Contractual and regulatory consequences may follow

Key Point:
Attackers do not need to breach a dealer directly if they can breach a vendor.

---

# 2. How Social Engineering Works (10 minutes)

## Social Engineering Defined

Manipulating people to bypass security controls.

It targets:
- Trust
- Urgency
- Authority
- Routine behavior

Most breaches begin with:
- A click
- A credential
- A payment change
- An access request

## Common Techniques (High-Level)

- Phishing (email, SMS, messaging apps)
- Pretexting (fabricated scenarios)
- Impersonation (executives, vendors, IT)
- Urgency/pressure tactics
- Conversation hijacking

Reinforce:
Familiar does not equal safe.

---

# 3. Additional Tactics You May Not Expect (10 minutes)

## Littering
- USB drives
- Printed "confidential" documents
- QR codes in shared spaces

## Cloning
- Lookalike invoices
- Duplicate login pages
- Slightly altered domains

## Conversation Hijacking
- Payment changes mid-thread
- “Updated” attachments
- Subtle instruction shifts

## Environmental Trust Exploitation
- Branded clothing
- Industry language
- Physical access assumptions

Key Point:
The more normal something feels, the more important verification becomes.

---

# 4. RICE: Recognizing Emotional Manipulation (10 minutes)

RICE stands for:
- Reward
- Intimidation
- Convenience
- Ego

Attackers trigger emotion to reduce critical thinking.

## Reward
- Bonuses
- Refunds
- Exclusive access

## Intimidation
- Account suspension
- Legal threats
- Compliance pressure

## Convenience
- “This will only take a minute”
- Bypassing ticketing or approval processes

## Ego
- Flattery
- Isolation (“Only you can handle this”)

Pause Signal:
If you feel:
- Urgency
- Fear
- Excitement
- Pressure
Stop and verify.

---

# 5. High-Risk Scenarios in Our Environment (10 minutes)

Focus on the most damaging realistic scenarios.

## Vendor Invoice / Payment Changes (Business Email Compromise)
- Updated wire instructions
- New ACH details
- “Send to this new address”

Always verify through a second channel.

---

## Platform Access & MFA Reset Requests
- “I’m locked out”
- “I need quick access”
- “Can you share credentials?”

Never share passwords.
Escalate reset requests through formal process.

---

## DMS Data Handling
- Unexpected export requests
- Large data pulls
- Requests for “raw lists”

Verify:
- Is this necessary?
- Is it approved?
- Is it consistent with safe harbor expectations?

---

## Executive Urgency Requests
- Gift cards
- Wire transfers
- Confidential document requests

Executives expect process compliance.

---

# 6. Data Classification & Safe Harbor (5 minutes)

Not all data is equal.

High sensitivity in our environment:
- Client DMS data
- Customer contact information
- Payment data
- Vendor contracts
- Access credentials

Do not:
- Upload client data to third-party analysis sites
- Share large exports without approval
- Email raw DMS data without authorization

If unsure:
Escalate before sharing.

---

# 7. What To Do (Operational Response Model) (5 minutes)

When something feels suspicious:

1. Pause
2. Verify (use known contact info, not reply-in-thread)
3. Escalate to security or leadership
4. Preserve evidence (do not delete immediately)

If you clicked:
Report immediately.
Speed matters more than embarrassment.

---

# 8. Cultural Expectations (5 minutes)

You are allowed to:
- Say no
- Ask questions
- Slow down
- Escalate

You will not be penalized for verifying.

Security is not about suspicion.
It is about protecting trust.

---

# Close

Three habits to remember:

Pause  
Verify  
Escalate  

Security awareness is operational maturity.
